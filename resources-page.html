SBA Checklist: Government Contracting — Where do I start?

MySetAside (MSA) has the opportunity to guide small businesses through their hiring process and specifically cater to small businesses. Helps you connect with federal and state agencies. The MSA database is in addition to other valuable resources such as grants.gov and System for Award Management (SAM) and General Services Administration (GSA).

The Grants.gov system contains information about veteran grant applications for over 1,000 grant programs and government grants that award more than $500 billion annually.

Federal business opportunities for contractors are listed at www.sam.gov. Federal agencies must use this website to inform the public and interested potential bidders of available procurement opportunities and their bidder requirements for all contracts over $25,000.

General Services Administration (GSA)

Checklist to help you prepare for bid on private and government procurement opportunities:

  • Get a PC and go online! Even if you do not have your own website, you still need at least an email address to do business with the government.
  • Find the DUNS number (Data Universal Numbering System). This number can be obtained by calling Dun and Bradstreet at 866-705-5711 or by visiting their website
  • Locate the NAICS (North American Industry Classification System) code. A code of conduct explains what your company does. Visit to find your NAICS code. Keep these codes handy as you may need them when completing the formal registration or when searching for offers.
  • Register your company with the System for Award Management (SAM, formerly Central Contracts Registration). Companies wishing to do business with the federalgovernment must register with SAM.
  • After registering for the SAM, complete the Online Representations and Certification Application (ORCA). This registration allows you to enter agent and certificate information for use in future government contracts.
  • After registering for the SAM, be sure to supply more information to register for the Dynamic Small Business Search. This database can be used by government contractors and prime contractors when searching for vendors.
  • Be sure to record the CAGE code. If you do not have a CAGE code, you will receive one when you sign up for SAM. The federal government can use this code to pay for goods and services.
  • Find your nearest Procurement Technical Assistance Center (PTAC). PTAC supplies local, personal advice and training services to small business owners. They are designed to supply technical aid to companies looking to sell products and services to federal, state, and/or local governments. PTAC services are available free or for a small fee. PTAC is part of the Procurement Technical Assistance Program administered by the Department of Defence. PTAC also supplies registration, answers to questions, and workshops.
  • Minority- and women-owned businesses must apply for certification through the State Offices of Minority and Women’s Business Enterprises (OMWBE). A list of SBA Government resources is available here. OMWBE certification helps your secure contracts with state and local government agencies and schools. Their special loan program has also lowered interest rates.
  • Your organization may qualify for his SBA procurement certifications such as HubZone and 8(a). The SBA Learning Center has many small business assessment tools and learning modules to meet your specific procurement certification needs. For example, Pre-8(a) Business Development Program Module 1 – Setting Expectations.
  • In addition, most federal agencies have public procurement specialists. Visit the Department of Commerce’s Small Business Activation Bureau OSDBU (Office of Small and Disadvantaged Business Utilization) to find agency representatives who work withsmall businesses.
  • Visit his website at SAM.gov. You can also register as a provider and send offers by email.
  • Visit the General Services Authority. Check out the GSA schedule to see the various goods and services listed. Consider applying for a GSA Agreement (called a “Schedule”). This allows government buyers to buy from you at pre-agreed prices.
  • Do not forget local government agencies and businesses. Check with local cities, ports, school districts, counties, and other local government agencies for information on buying practices, small work rosters, vendor lists, and more.
What is the SBA Sizing Standard?

The SBA sets the industry standard for small business size. Uses the North American Industry Classification System (NAICS) codes to find various industries. NAICS classifies all economic activity into 20 sectors.
